I'm a computational physicist, specializing in high-energy astrophysics.

Most recently, I worked as a project scientist at Berkeley Lab.

My research in computational astrophysics includes algorithms for nuclear reactions, solving PDEs on adaptive meshes, and neutrino quantum kinetics. I also develop methods for accelerating these algorithms for GPU-based supercomputers as well as ML surrogate models.

Recently, I also worked on mesoscale atmospheric modeling as part of the Energy Research and Forecasting project at LBL.

Nowadays, I'm interested in ...

  • adaptively accurate methods for multiphysics modules in our large-scale codes
  • extending our ML surrogate models for nucleosynthesis to wider thermodynamic ranges
  • mathematical techniques for using our forward simulations to solve inverse science problems
